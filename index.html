The Impact of Carbon Capture and Storage

Carbon capture and storage (CCS) is a critical technology in the fight against climate change. By capturing carbon dioxide emissions from sources such as power plants and industrial processes, and storing it underground, CCS prevents significant amounts of CO2 from entering the atmosphere.

Implementing CCS at a large scale can greatly contribute to achieving net-zero emissions by offsetting emissions that are difficult to eliminate. This technology not only helps in reducing atmospheric CO2 levels but also in stabilizing global temperatures, protecting ecosystems, and supporting sustainable development goals.

With ongoing research and advancements, CCS is becoming more efficient and cost-effective, making it a viable option for countries aiming to meet their climate targets. As we continue to innovate and improve these technologies, we move closer to a sustainable and carbon-neutral future.
